Introduction

Developed as part of the Knowledge Engineering for the Humanities (KE4H) course at , University of Bologna. this project investigates the integration of SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs). Our primary objective was to determine how this combination can be leveraged to enrich an existing cultural heritage knowledge graph with meaningful new content.

We chose to focus on Villa Carlotta, a historic villa on the shores of Lake Como that today hosts exhibitions, concerts, ballets and publications. The villa is of particular interest to our team, as many of us aspire to careers in international event management, and Villa Carlotta is a great example of cultural and historical heritage being used to host modern themed events.

As our base knowledge graph, we selected ArCo — the Italian Ministry of Culture’s official RDF dataset and ontology network—as our base knowledge graph. ArCo provides a well-structured, detailed representation of Italy’s cultural heritage and an excellent SPARQL endpoint for semantic queries. Its modular architecture and domain-specific ontologies made it the perfect fit for our project, especially for exploring tangible heritage.
