Introduction

This project was developed within the framework of the KE4H (Knowledge Engineering for the Humanities) course at the University of Bologna. Our goal was to explore how the combination of SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs) can enrich an existing cultural heritage knowledge graph with new, meaningful content.

We chose to focus on Villa Carlotta, a historic villa on the shores of Lake Como that today hosts exhibitions, concerts, ballets and publications. The villa is of particular interest to our team, as many of us aspire to careers in international event management, and Villa Carlotta is a great example of cultural and historical heritage being used to host modern themed events.

As our base knowledge graph, we selected ArCo — the official ontology network and RDF dataset of the Italian Ministry of Culture. ArCo offers a rich and well-structured representation of Italy's cultural heritage and provides an excellent SPARQL endpoint for advanced semantic exploration. Its modular architecture and domain-specific ontologies made it a strong choice for our project's objectives, especially in the context of tangible heritage.
